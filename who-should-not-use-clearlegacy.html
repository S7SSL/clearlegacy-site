When a solicitor is the better choice

An online will is built for straightforward estates. If any of the following apply to you, a private-client solicitor or STEP practitioner is worth the extra cost — and we'd genuinely rather you used one:

  • Complex trusts — discretionary trusts, disabled or vulnerable beneficiaries, or trusts to control how and when assets are received.
  • International or cross-border estates — property abroad, foreign tax exposure, or assets in multiple jurisdictions.
  • Business succession — passing on a company, partnership shares or business property relief planning.
  • Very high-net-worth inheritance-tax planning — estates well into seven figures needing structured IHT mitigation.
  • Likely disputes — estranged family, second marriages with competing claims, or a realistic risk your will is challenged.
  • Concerns about mental capacity — if there's any doubt about capacity, a solicitor can assess and evidence it properly.
Why we tell you this

Selling a £69 will to someone who needs £2,000 of solicitor advice would be a disservice — and a will that doesn't fit your situation can cause real harm. Honesty is the point.

FAQs

Is ClearLegacy legitimate?

Yes. ClearLegacy produces wills that are legally valid in England & Wales when signed and witnessed correctly, at a fixed price with secure Stripe checkout. We also tell you before you start if your situation needs a solicitor instead.

Why would a will company tell me not to use it?

Because the right will matters more than a sale. If your estate is complex, an off-the-shelf online will can do more harm than good — so we point you to a solicitor. For the majority with straightforward estates, an online will is the sensible, affordable choice.

How do I know if my estate is 'complex'?

Broadly: trusts beyond a simple gift to under-18s, foreign assets, a business to pass on, very large IHT planning, or a likely dispute. If you start our questionnaire and your situation flags as complex, we'll tell you before you pay.
