About SL

SL is the founder of ClearLegacy and writes most of the guides on this site. The reason the business exists is straightforward: writing a basic, legally valid will in the UK shouldn't cost £200–£400. Most estates are not complicated. Most of the cost in the high-street model is office overhead and hourly billing, not legal work.

ClearLegacy uses a structured questionnaire that walks through the same questions a solicitor would ask — executors, beneficiaries, residuary estate, guardians for children — and produces a will drafted to the requirements of the Wills Act 1837. Delivery is automated end-to-end, which is why we can charge £69 for a single will and £99 for mirror wills.

Background

SL's background is in digital services and estate planning. ClearLegacy was set up after seeing how many people put off writing a will entirely because the high-street quote felt disproportionate to the size of their estate. The intent of every guide on this site is the same: explain a real UK rule clearly, without padding, so that someone with no legal background can make an informed decision.

How SL writes and reviews content

Every guide on ClearLegacy is researched against current UK primary sources — GOV.UK, HMRC, Citizens Advice, the Office of the Public Guardian, and the underlying statute. Thresholds and figures are checked at publication and re-checked when the rules change (for example, IHT thresholds are now frozen until 2030; APR and BPR caps change in April 2026).

The full editorial standard is documented on the editorial policy page. The end-to-end process for how ClearLegacy wills themselves are produced and quality-checked is documented on the review process page.

FAQs

Who writes ClearLegacy's content?

SL, the founder of ClearLegacy, is the lead author and reviewer of all guides and legal explainers on the site. Content is researched against primary UK sources including GOV.UK, HMRC, Citizens Advice and the Wills Act 1837.

Is SL a solicitor?

No. SL is the founder of an online will-writing service, not a practising solicitor. ClearLegacy's wills are generated from a structured questionnaire that follows the Wills Act 1837. For complex estates — discretionary trusts, family business shares, foreign property — we recommend a solicitor.

How is content reviewed before publication?

Every guide is checked against current UK statute and GOV.UK guidance before publication, and re-checked when thresholds or rules change. The review process is documented at /review-process/ and the editorial standards at /editorial-policy/.
