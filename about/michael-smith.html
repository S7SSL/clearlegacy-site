Michael Smith is the estate planning specialist responsible for reviewing ClearLegacy's educational content. He checks the knowledge base, scenario library, decision trees and data pages for legal accuracy and clarity before publication, and signs off the review date shown on each page.

Areas of focus

  • Wills, will validity and execution under the Wills Act 1837
  • Probate and estate administration
  • Inheritance tax thresholds, reliefs and exemptions
  • Executors' duties and powers
  • Intestacy and the rights of unmarried partners and stepchildren
How ClearLegacy content is reviewed

Every knowledge-base, scenario, decision-tree and data page is written in plain English and checked against primary UK sources — including legislation on legislation.gov.uk, guidance on GOV.UK, and published figures from HMRC, the Ministry of Justice and the ONS. Each page shows a "Last reviewed" and "Next review" date, and is revisited on that schedule so the information stays current as thresholds, fees and rules change.

This content provides general legal information, not legal advice. Estate planning depends on individual circumstances — readers should speak to a qualified solicitor or STEP practitioner before acting.
