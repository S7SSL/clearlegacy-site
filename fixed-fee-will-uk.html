What "Fixed Fee" Actually Means

"Fixed fee" sounds straightforward but is used loosely in the will writing market. A genuine fixed fee should mean:

  • The price is agreed before any work begins
  • It covers drafting, compliance review and delivery
  • There are no hourly add-ons if the work takes longer than expected
  • There are no annual fees for storage, "membership" or rewrite cover
  • You can make changes before you sign without being charged again

If any of those caveats apply, the headline fee is misleading. ClearLegacy's £69 and £99 prices include all of the above.

Hidden Costs to Watch For

Some "fixed fee" wills come with surprises buried in the small print. Common traps:

  • Annual storage fee — £20–£40 per year to keep the original at the will writer's office. Over 30 years that adds up to more than the will cost.
  • "Free will" with a trust upsell — the headline will is free but you are sold a discretionary trust for £500+ that you almost certainly do not need
  • Subscription wills — £15–£25 per month for "rewrite cover" most people never use
  • Amendment charges — £50–£200 each time you tweak something before signing
  • Trust corporation appointments — bundling in a paid trustee that takes a percentage of your estate when you die

Read the price page carefully and ignore the headline. The honest comparison is the lifetime cost.

Why Fixed Fee Works for Standard Wills

The case for fixed pricing on standard wills is simple: most wills follow the same structure. Executors, guardians, specific gifts, residuary estate. The legal wording is largely standardised. Once a service has the questionnaire and drafting workflow in place, the marginal cost of producing each will is tiny — and the savings can be passed on as a transparent fixed price.

Hourly billing made sense when wills were typed by hand from notes after a meeting. It makes much less sense in 2026 when a guided online questionnaire collects the data and a templated drafting engine produces the document.

When You Should Pay Hourly Instead

Fixed-fee online wills work for most UK estates but not all. Hourly solicitor advice is genuinely worth the unpredictable bill if you:

  • Need a discretionary or life-interest trust drafted
  • Are planning business succession for a company you control
  • Hold significant property abroad
  • Expect the will to be challenged
  • Are using your will as part of an inheritance tax strategy
