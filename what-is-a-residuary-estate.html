What is a residuary estate?

England & Wales · Wills

Quick answer

The residuary estate is everything left over once debts, taxes, funeral costs, expenses and all specific gifts have been dealt with. The person who inherits it is the residuary beneficiary. A residuary clause is essential in any will — without one, anything not specifically gifted passes under the intestacy rules (a “partial intestacy”).

Detailed explanation

Think of your estate as a series of layers. The residue is the bottom layer — and usually the biggest.

How the estate is paid out

  1. Debts, taxes, funeral and administration costs are paid first.
  2. Specific and pecuniary gifts (named items and fixed sums) are made next.
  3. Whatever remains — the residue — goes to the residuary beneficiary.

Why the residuary clause matters

If your will makes specific gifts but doesn't say who gets the rest, the leftover passes under intestacy — possibly to people you didn't intend. A residuary clause captures everything not otherwise dealt with, including assets you acquire after making the will.

Example scenario

Bola's will leaves £5,000 to a friend and her car to her brother, then leaves “the residue of my estate to my two children in equal shares”. That residuary clause captures her house, savings and everything else — including a bonus she received after writing the will.

Never leave a gap. A clear residuary clause is what stops part of your estate slipping into intestacy.

Frequently asked questions

What is a residuary estate?

The residuary estate is everything left after debts, taxes, expenses and specific gifts have been paid. It passes to the residuary beneficiary named in the will. It is usually the largest part of an estate.

What is a residuary beneficiary?

A residuary beneficiary is the person (or people, or charity) who inherits the residue of the estate — whatever remains after all debts, taxes and specific gifts. Many wills leave the residue to a spouse or to children in equal shares.

What happens if a will has no residuary clause?

Anything not covered by a specific gift passes under the intestacy rules — a partial intestacy — which may send it to people the testator did not intend. A residuary clause prevents this.
