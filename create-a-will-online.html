What happens if you don’t have a will

The risk
  • If you die without a will, the intestacy rules — not you — decide who inherits.
  • An unmarried partner inherits nothing automatically, however long you've been together.
  • Children inherit outright at 18, with guardianship left to the courts.
  • Your family faces extra cost, delay and uncertainty at the hardest time.

What you can do in your online will

  • Leave your estate to the partner, children, family or causes you choose.
  • Appoint executors you trust to carry out your wishes.
  • Name guardians for any children under 18.
  • Add specific gifts and funeral wishes if you want to.

Frequently asked questions

Can I create a legally valid will online?

Yes. A will created online is legally valid in England & Wales provided you sign it and have it witnessed correctly by two independent adults. ClearLegacy gives you clear signing and witnessing instructions.

How much does it cost to create a will online?

A single will is a fixed £69 and mirror wills for a couple are £99. There is no hourly billing and no hidden upsells, and your first amendment is included.

How long does it take?

Most people answer the questions in under half an hour, and your finished will is emailed to you within 24 hours, ready to print, sign and witness.

Do I need a solicitor to make a will?

For a straightforward estate, no — a guided online will covers most situations at a fraction of solicitor fees. If your circumstances are complex (trusts, business assets, foreign property), we will flag that before you start.
