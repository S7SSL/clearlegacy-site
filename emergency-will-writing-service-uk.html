How do I get an emergency will written in the UK?

England & Wales · Urgent Wills

Quick answer

In an emergency, make a valid will the same day: complete a guided online will, print it, and sign it with two independent witnesses present together — it's valid immediately. If you're seriously ill and can't sign normally, someone can sign at your direction in your presence. For complex estates, get a simple valid will in place now and a solicitor's help straight after.

Detailed explanation

An emergency will is about getting valid cover in place fast.

  • Write it online, print it, sign with two independent witnesses present together.
  • If you physically can't sign, another person may sign in your presence and at your direction.
  • Make it while you clearly have capacity.
  • Tell someone trusted where the signed will is.

For deathbed or hospital situations, a solicitor experienced in urgent wills can attend — but a correctly signed will made today is valid now.

Example scenario

With a relative gravely ill in hospital, the family helps them complete a simple will expressing clear wishes; the patient directs a non-beneficiary to sign on their behalf in their presence, with two independent witnesses — a valid emergency will.

Sensitive situations: for serious illness, capacity and undue-influence concerns matter. Where possible, involve a solicitor experienced in urgent wills alongside making a valid will now.
