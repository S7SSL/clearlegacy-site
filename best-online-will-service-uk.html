What "Best" Should Mean in 2026

The online will market is crowded, and "best" reviews online are often built around affiliate commissions rather than what works for the person making the will. The criteria that actually matter:

  1. Legal validity — the finished document must comply with the Wills Act 1837
  2. Transparent fixed pricing — the price you see is the price you pay, no hourly billing, no surprise extras
  3. Coverage of all standard clauses — executors, guardians, specific gifts, residue, substitutes, revocation, funeral wishes
  4. Plain English — questions you can understand, not legal jargon
  5. Compliance review — a human or automated check before delivery
  6. Clear signing instructions — most DIY failures happen at the signing step
  7. No aggressive upsells — beware free-will-with-paid-trust bundles, subscription billing, and storage fees
  8. Speed — under 30 minutes to complete, delivery within 24–48 hours

What to Avoid in an Online Will Service

  • Mandatory storage fees — keep the original at home in a fireproof box, or with your executor
  • Subscription wills — most people never use the rewrite cover they're paying monthly for
  • Free-will-with-paid-trust bundles — discretionary trusts are oversold and rarely necessary for ordinary estates
  • Trust corporation appointments — bundling in a paid trustee that takes a percentage of your estate
  • "Instant" wills with no compliance review — the review is what catches the most expensive mistakes
  • Services that store your sensitive data offshore — UK GDPR matters for a document this personal

When an Online Service Isn't Right for You

Even the best online will service is not the right tool for every estate. Use a specialist solicitor if you:

  • Want to set up a discretionary or life-interest trust
  • Are planning business succession
  • Hold significant property abroad
  • Expect the will to be challenged
  • Are using your will for inheritance tax planning
