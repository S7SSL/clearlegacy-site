3. Why we use your data (lawful basis)

We rely on the following lawful bases under UK GDPR Article 6:

  • Contract — to prepare your will or LPA documents, take payment, deliver outputs and provide customer support.
  • Legal obligation — to keep records for HMRC (six years), comply with anti-money-laundering checks, and respond to lawful information requests.
  • Legitimate interests — to operate and improve the website, prevent fraud, measure marketing performance, and contact you about your order.
  • Consent — for marketing emails and non-essential cookies (you can withdraw at any time).

5. How long we keep your data

  • Will and LPA documents — stored securely for 90 days after delivery so you can re-download. After 90 days the digital draft is deleted unless you have requested extended storage.
  • Order and payment records — six years from the end of the financial year the transaction relates to (HMRC requirement).
  • Marketing data — until you unsubscribe.
  • Website analytics — anonymised after 14 months (GA4 default).
  • Support correspondence — three years from your last interaction.

6. Cookies and tracking

We use the following cookies and similar technologies:

  • Essential — session management, security, payment processing. These cannot be turned off.
  • Analytics — Google Analytics 4 to understand which pages perform and how visitors find us.
  • Advertising — Microsoft UET and Google Ads conversion tags to measure ad campaign performance.

You can control non-essential cookies through your browser settings or our cookie banner where shown. Blocking analytics cookies will not affect your ability to use the service.

7. Your rights

Under UK GDPR you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Have inaccurate data corrected
  • Have your data erased (subject to our legal retention obligations)
  • Restrict or object to certain types of processing
  • Receive your data in a portable format
  • Withdraw consent for marketing at any time
  • Lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office

To exercise any of these rights email hello@clearlegacy.co.uk. We will respond within one month.

8. Security

We protect your data with TLS 1.2+ in transit, encryption at rest, access controls limited to a small number of authorised personnel, and regular review of our processors. Card payments are handled exclusively by Stripe and never touch our servers.

If you believe your data has been compromised, contact us immediately at hello@clearlegacy.co.uk. We will investigate and notify the ICO within 72 hours where required.

9. Children

Our service is intended for adults aged 18 and over. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has provided us with information, contact us and we will delete it.

10. Complaints

If you are unhappy with how we have handled your personal data, please contact us first at hello@clearlegacy.co.uk so we can put it right. You also have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner's Office at any time.

11. Changes to this policy

We will update this page when our practices change. The date at the top reflects the most recent revision. Material changes will be communicated by email where we hold consent.
