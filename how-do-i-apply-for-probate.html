How do I apply for probate?

England & Wales · Probate

Quick answer

First value the estate and deal with any inheritance tax. Then apply for the grant — usually online through the GOV.UK probate service (or by post) — submitting the original will, the death certificate and the £300 fee (estates over £5,000). Once issued, the grant proves your authority to collect in and distribute the estate.

Detailed explanation

The order matters: tax before grant, grant before distribution.

  1. Value the estate — assets and debts at the date of death.
  2. Report inheritance tax to HMRC and pay any due (or the first instalment).
  3. Apply for the grant online or by post, with the will and death certificate.
  4. Pay the £300 fee; order extra copies at £16 each.
  5. Swear/confirm the application truthfully; the grant is then issued (around 5 weeks).
Example scenario

After valuing his late mother's £310,000 estate and confirming no inheritance tax is due, Sam applies online, uploads details from the will and death certificate, pays £300, and receives the grant about five weeks later.
