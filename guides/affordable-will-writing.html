Is a £69 Will Actually Legal?

Yes. The legal validity of a will depends on proper drafting and correct witnessing — not on the fee you pay. A will must be signed by the testator in the presence of two independent witnesses who also sign. ClearLegacy wills meet all requirements of the Wills Act 1837.

Who Should Use an Affordable Will Service?

An affordable online will service is suitable for most people with straightforward estates. If you want to name beneficiaries, appoint executors, specify guardians for children, and distribute your assets clearly, a ClearLegacy will covers all of this.

If your situation involves complex trusts, foreign assets, or business succession planning, you may need specialist advice in addition to your will.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I update my will later?

Yes. ClearLegacy includes one free amendment. We recommend reviewing your will every 3–5 years or after major life events such as marriage, divorce, or the birth of a child.

Do I need a solicitor to write a will?

No. There is no legal requirement to use a solicitor. What matters is that the will is correctly drafted, properly signed, and witnessed by two independent adults.

How do Mirror Wills work?

Mirror wills are two matching wills for couples who want to leave their estate to each other. ClearLegacy offers mirror wills for £99 — both wills are delivered together within 24 hours.
