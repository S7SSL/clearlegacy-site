How ClearLegacy works

England & Wales · How it works · ClearLegacy

Quick answer

ClearLegacy works in five steps: complete a guided questionnaire, your answers are automatically reviewed, you receive your documents, you sign them correctly with our instructions, and your family is protected by a valid will instead of the intestacy default. The whole process is online and built for England & Wales.

The ClearLegacy process, step by step

The process is designed to be simple enough to finish in one sitting, with safeguards built in.

Step 1 — Complete the questionnaire

Answer guided questions about your family, your assets and your wishes. We prompt you on the decisions people often forget — guardians for children, substitute beneficiaries, and what happens to the residue.

Step 2 — Automated review

Your answers are checked by our automated review pipeline for completeness and obvious gaps before anything is finalised.

Step 3 — Receive your documents

You get your will and supporting paperwork, ready to print.

Step 4 — Sign correctly

We give clear instructions on signing and witnessing under section 9 of the Wills Act 1837 — two independent witnesses, present together — so the will is legally valid.

Step 5 — Protect your family

Your wishes are recorded, your executors know what to do, and your loved ones are spared the uncertainty of intestacy.

Start here

Many people begin with the free Estate Risk Assessment to see their specific gaps, then complete the will questionnaire the same evening — turning months of “I really should sort this out” into a finished, valid will.

Witnessing matters: a will that isn't signed and witnessed correctly can be invalid. Our step-4 instructions are there precisely to prevent that common, costly mistake.
