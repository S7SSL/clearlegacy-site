2. Charity Will Schemes

Will Aid

Every November, hundreds of UK solicitors take part in Will Aid. They waive their fee in exchange for a suggested donation to a charity coalition (currently around £100 for a single will, £180 for mirror wills). The will is drafted to solicitor standard. The downsides are that slots fill within days of opening and you have to attend in person.

Free Wills Month

Free Wills Month runs in March and October for people aged 55 and over. Participating solicitors waive their fee, with the participating charities hoping you may include a legacy in your will (you are not obligated to). Availability is limited and books up quickly.

Other schemes

Some charities run year-round will-writing schemes through partner solicitors — Cancer Research UK, the National Trust and the RNLI all offer something similar. The model is the same: free or discounted will in exchange for considering a charity legacy.

Charity will schemes are great if you can get a slot. If you cannot — or your case is more urgent than the next scheme month — an online will is usually faster and not far behind on price.

3. Trade Union and Employer Schemes

Many UK trade unions (Unite, Unison, GMB and others) include free or heavily discounted wills as a member benefit. Some employers offer the same through workplace benefits providers. If you are a member, this is often the cheapest legitimate route — though the panel solicitors involved may have caps on what they will cover for free.

4. DIY Will Templates — Avoid Unless You Really Know What You're Doing

Free or low-cost will templates are available from supermarkets, banks and consumer sites. They are also the most common source of invalid wills at probate. The most common failures:

  • Missing residuary clause — that part of the estate falls under intestacy
  • Beneficiaries acting as witnesses — voids the gift to that beneficiary
  • Ambiguous wording — "to my children" without naming them
  • Improper signing — only one witness, or witnesses not present at the same time

For an extra £40–£60 over a DIY template, a guided online service prompts you for every required clause and won't let you skip the bits that matter.

What to Avoid

The "affordable" end of the will market is also where most of the worst products live. Specifically:

  • "Free will" with a trust upsell — the headline will is free, but you are pressured into a £500+ discretionary trust you almost certainly do not need
  • Subscription wills — £15–£25 per month for "rewrite cover" most people never use
  • Annual storage fees — £20–£40 per year that quietly exceed the original will price over time
  • Trust corporation appointments — bundling in a paid trustee that takes a percentage of your estate
  • Doorstep will writers — high-pressure home visits, often pushing add-on products

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the most affordable way to write a will in the UK?

A guided online will writing service from £69, or a charity will scheme such as Will Aid for a suggested donation. DIY templates are technically cheaper but error-prone.

How do charity will schemes work?

Will Aid (November) and Free Wills Month (March / October) pair you with a participating solicitor who waives their fee in exchange for a donation. Slots are limited and book up quickly.

Are affordable wills as legally valid as expensive ones?

Yes. Legal validity is set by the Wills Act 1837 and does not depend on price. A £69 online will and a £500 solicitor will are equally valid as long as both are signed and witnessed correctly.

What should I avoid when looking for a cheap will?

Headline "free will" offers bundled with a discretionary trust upsell, subscription wills, annual storage fees, and DIY templates if you have any complexity. See our cheap will UK guide for the full picture.

Is an online will safe for a complete estate?

Yes — for the standard estate of a home, savings, pensions, family beneficiaries and named guardians. Use a specialist solicitor for trusts, business succession, overseas assets, or anticipated will challenges.
