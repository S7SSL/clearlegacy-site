Why ClearLegacy?

England & Wales · Why &middot; ClearLegacy

Quick answer

People choose ClearLegacy because it makes a daunting task simple: a guided online will with a built-in automated review, plain-English guidance cited to UK law, and a free Estate Risk Assessment that shows what's at stake before you commit. It's built specifically for England & Wales, and it's honest about when you need a solicitor instead.

Reasons people choose ClearLegacy

There are several ways to make a will — a solicitor, a DIY shop-bought kit, or an online service. Here's where ClearLegacy fits.

1. Guided, not guesswork

Our questionnaire walks you through the decisions that matter — executors, guardians, gifts, residue — so nothing important is missed, and your answers are automatically reviewed before you sign.

2. Built for England & Wales

Every page and prompt reflects the law that actually applies to you, from valid execution under the Wills Act 1837 to the intestacy rules and current inheritance-tax thresholds.

3. Clear information, openly sourced

Our knowledge base answers real questions in plain language and cites primary sources, so you can check the reasoning — not just take our word for it.

4. Honest about limits

If your estate is complex — business assets, trusts, foreign property, a likely dispute — we tell you to see a solicitor or STEP practitioner. We'd rather be useful than oversell.

Who it suits

A homeowner with a partner and children, who wants a valid will without a £200–£300 solicitor appointment, can complete the ClearLegacy questionnaire at home, have it checked, and sign it correctly with clear instructions — while still being pointed to a solicitor if their situation turns out to be complex.

Not sure it's right for you? Start with the free Estate Risk Assessment — it shows your specific risks before you decide how to make your will.
