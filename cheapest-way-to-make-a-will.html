What is the cheapest way to make a will?

England & Wales · Urgent Wills · Cost

Quick answer

The cheapest valid routes are a low-cost online will service or a DIY will kit. A DIY kit is cheapest of all, but carries the highest risk of wording or witnessing errors that make the will invalid. A guided online service like ClearLegacy keeps costs low while adding an automated review, balancing price against the risk of getting it wrong.

Detailed explanation

Cheapest isn't always best value — an invalid will costs your family far more later.

  • DIY kit: lowest cost, highest risk of invalidity.
  • Online will service: low cost, guided, often with a review step.
  • Solicitor: highest cost, best for complex estates.
  • Free will schemes exist (e.g. some charity months) but may expect a donation.
Example scenario

On a tight budget, Leah uses a guided online will rather than a bare DIY template. It costs a little more than a kit, but the review step catches a missing residuary clause — saving her family a partial intestacy.

Frequently asked questions

What is the cheapest way to make a will?

A low-cost online will service or a DIY kit. DIY is cheapest but riskiest, as wording and witnessing errors can invalidate it. A guided online service keeps costs low while adding a review to catch mistakes.

Is a cheap will still legally valid?

Yes, if it meets the rules — in writing, signed, and witnessed by two independent people present together. The risk with the very cheapest DIY options is errors that make it invalid, not the low price itself.
