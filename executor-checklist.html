Executor checklist

England & Wales · Executors &middot; Checklist

Quick answer

Work through it in order: register the death, locate the will, secure the assets, value the estate, report inheritance tax, apply for the grant of probate, collect in the assets, pay debts and expenses, prepare estate accounts, and distribute to beneficiaries. Keep records at every step — you may have to account for them later.

Your executor checklist, in order

  1. Register the death and get certified copies of the certificate.
  2. Locate the original will and confirm you are the named executor.
  3. Secure assets — insure empty property, notify banks, redirect post.
  4. Value the estate at the date of death — assets and debts.
  5. Report inheritance tax to HMRC; pay any tax due before applying.
  6. Apply for the grant of probate (£300; £16 per copy).
  7. Collect in assets — close accounts, sell or transfer property.
  8. Pay debts, the funeral, and final income tax.
  9. Place statutory creditor notices to protect yourself.
  10. Prepare estate accounts and, after the six-month claim window, distribute.

Tackle the valuation thoroughly — it drives both the tax position and the probate application, and errors here cause the most delay.

Common pitfall

An executor pays out the full estate four months after the grant, then a creditor appears. Because they hadn't placed creditor notices or waited out the claim window, they face the shortfall personally. Following the checklist's protective steps (notices, waiting period) would have prevented it.

Beneficiaries can ask for the accounts. Tidy, contemporaneous records are your best defence and make distribution far smoother.
