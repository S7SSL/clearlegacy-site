Why Cheap Doesn't Mean Low Quality

The cost of a will reflects the delivery model, not the document itself. Solicitor pricing covers face-to-face appointments, office overheads, and time spent typing your details into a system. An online service automates everything that doesn't actually need a human, then has the draft reviewed for compliance.

The finished will is governed by the same Wills Act 1837 regardless of how much you paid. As long as it's:

  • In writing
  • Signed by you
  • Witnessed by two adults present at the same time who aren't beneficiaries

...it has full legal effect. The cheapest will and the most expensive will give your executor the same authority and your beneficiaries the same rights.

The HMRC and probate registry don't ask what you paid. They check whether the document complies with the Wills Act 1837. Price is irrelevant to validity.

Why "Cheap" Beats "DIY"

If price is the main concern, a £15 DIY will kit looks tempting. The trouble is what happens next:

  • Missing residuary clause — that part of your estate then passes under intestacy
  • Beneficiary witnesses — voids the gift to that beneficiary entirely
  • Ambiguous wording — "to the kids" without naming them, or naming a charity by an old name
  • No substitute beneficiaries — gifts fail if the primary beneficiary dies first
  • Improper signing — most common reason DIY wills are rejected at probate

A guided online service prompts you for every required clause and won't let you skip the bits that matter. For an extra £40–£60 over a DIY kit, the risk of failure drops dramatically.

When to Spend More on a Will

An online will isn't right for every situation. Consider a specialist solicitor (£500+) if:

  • You have offshore assets or property abroad in multiple jurisdictions
  • You want to set up complex trusts — discretionary, life interest, or disabled beneficiary trusts
  • Your will involves business succession planning for a company you control
  • You expect the will to be contested — estranged family, second marriages with stepchildren
  • Your estate is large enough that inheritance tax planning drives the structure

For everyone else — and that's the majority of UK adults — a £69 online will does the job.
