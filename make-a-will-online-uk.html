Make a Will Online in the UK

Updated May 2026 · 6 min read

Yes — you can make a legally valid will entirely online in the UK. As long as it is properly signed and witnessed under the Wills Act 1837, an online will carries exactly the same legal weight as one drafted by a solicitor. ClearLegacy will start at £69 and most people finish in under 30 minutes.

Is an Online Will Legally Valid?

Yes. The law that governs wills in England and Wales is the Wills Act 1837, and it does not specify who must draft the document. What it does specify is how the will must be executed:

  • The will must be in writing
  • You must be 18 or over and have mental capacity
  • You must sign the will, or direct someone to sign it on your behalf
  • Two adult witnesses must be present at the same time when you sign
  • Those witnesses must then sign in your presence
  • Witnesses cannot be beneficiaries or married to a beneficiary

An online will service produces the document, but the signing and witnessing still happens in person. That is the only legal requirement — and exactly the same step a solicitor-drafted will has to follow.

The myth: "Online wills aren't proper wills." The reality: the law has nothing to say about how a will is drafted. A guided online questionnaire produces the same wording, the same clauses, and the same legal effect as a solicitor would.
