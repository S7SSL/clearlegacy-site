The honest truth about starting a Will

Most people put off making a Will not because it's complicated, but because it feels heavy. ClearLegacy's questionnaire is designed to remove that friction. You start, you answer the questions in plain English, you submit. Most people finish in one sitting because the questions are short and clear. If you don't, your progress saves automatically and you can come back later.

What you actually need before you start

  • Your full legal name, date of birth and home address
  • The names and rough addresses of the people you'd like to leave things to
  • Some idea of who you'd want as executors — typically a spouse, adult child, or trusted friend
  • If you have children under 18: who you'd like as guardians
  • About 15 minutes of uninterrupted time
